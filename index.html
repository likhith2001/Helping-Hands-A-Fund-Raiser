ABOUT US

Charity is essential and therefore meant to be done for public benefit, relief and to provide assistance to people at times of need in any part of the world, especially those who are the victims of war, natural disaster, catastrophe, hunger, disease, poverty, orphans by supplying them with food, shelter, medical aid, and other fundamental needs. Such charitable purposes can gain momentum from advancing the education of young people for the public benefit by making grants and awards to students in full-time education. When considering poverty in the developing world, people feel deep sorrow but seem to put no effort whatsoever to reduce or eradicate the problem. Poverty in today’s world has turned out to be sinister and we lay passive towards the problem, therefore such attitude has made us powerless to stop it. Since this world has been created it’s been unequal in every way and there will always be rich and poor, strong and weak living together. While poverty is extreme and widespread, let’s not forget just how many rich and wealthy people there are in the developed world capable of giving. It’s high time we must realize the power that we have in our hands when pooled together.

PAST CAMPAIGNS

COVID
Helping hands vocational training centres and community empowerment campaigns responded to this humanitarian crisis by providing daily wage workers and labourers with food assistance, such as cooked food, dry rations, vegetables and other daily essentials, and sanitation kits that include masks, gloves, sanitisers and soaps. Through this campaign, this organisation provided 1000 people with cooked food, 700 with dry ration and grocery kits, 500 with sanitation kits and 800 people were supported through tele-counselling and 1,000 patients were given treatment through mobile clinics.
CAMPAIGN 2
A campaign organised by Helping Hands against the child labour and supporting child education was conducted in the month of January, 2019. Poor and uneducated children, their parents, teachers, Helping hands volunteers, few businessman and government officials actively took part in the campaign. We marched across the city spreading awareness and shouting slogans like, “Put them in classes, not in tea glasses”. And we were really happy and successful in receiving both financial and non-financial support from people.
CAMPAIGN 3
Helping Hands has initiated social media campaign for women empowerment “LikeAGirl”, which is aimed at providing self-employment opportunities to women in varied sectors such as food packaging, tailoring, factory work, data entry, agricultural field work etc., that can make them financially independent.
CAMPAIGN 4
Single parents face discrimination throughout their daily lives, from the workplace to accessing housing. This has knock on effects for them, their children and society as a whole. Keeping this in mind, Helping hands had framed a petition that would support and help the single parents. The petition is signed by around 50,000 people as of now.
